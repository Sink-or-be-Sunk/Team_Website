This week I switched gears to focus fully on helping the team to complete our schematics/layouts for both of our boards. We have two types of board, the Grid board and the ESP board. The later of the two (as the name suggests) holds the microcontroller and all main circuitry including power regulation, charging, and programming ports. Additionally, the ESP board holds many connects to the various components of the design including the speaker, rumble motor, keypad, and lcd.

The other board in our design is the Grid board. This is an auxiliary board whose job is to simply house the LEDs and detect the placement of ships. This board holds very few components. The board holds a multiplexer to route the signals from each of the ship detection rows back to the ESP board. Additionally, the board routes the daisy chained LED ICs.
